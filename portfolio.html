TSA - Toronto School of art

www.tsa-art.com

The Toronto School of Art is an art school that teaches all experience levels. The purpose of this new website was to allow future students to directly purchase classes without the need of a third party website. This gave us the creative opportunity to build an entirely new website.

When the page loads, all of the elements take their place one by one. The TSA (logo?) and the painting behind it move when the cursor is moved, and follow the user’s movement down the homepage.

On the courses page, side navigation is fixed, staying with the user the entire time they’re on the website. This helps users navigate through the available courses displayed through their selected term and course-type filters.

Side navigation works the same way on this page, helping the user navigate between sections. As well, we added a filtering system on the top of the sections, allowing the user to choose the level of their course and the duration. This removes the need to go back to the previous page to change the filter selections.

This page is for single courses. The user can pick the time frame that they want to choose from, and see what will be taught.

The cart page allows users to input their information, while also allowing them to remove or add a course, as well as adding an additional student to a course.

The user can also see their total, and input any promo codes should they have any.

Meridian Main Website

This project is one of the biggest that I’ve worked on. It was for a Credit Union, and we wanted to make them different from the "Big 5" Canadian banks. We pitched 3 different layouts which you will see below.

The welcome section has a cinematography as the background. Users can access their accounts directly from this section, and if they want to access the menu, they can access it from the menu button at the top.

When the user starts scrolling, the navigation sticks to the top of the page, so it’s always there to help.

Meridian is a company that values their community and customers, so instead of filling the sections with a lot of information about their products or services, we went with placing community news on the homepage.

This is another concept that plays it safe. It is clean and gives a little more information about the services Meridian provides.

The third concept uses the fixed, full height, left navigation style that remains open. This style is a combination of the previous two. Because navigation is on the left, we have the full content area available for actual content.

Cundari Internal App

This was a spec execution that I worked on in my spare time, and it turned out to be a portfolio-worthy piece. My main reason for making this was that I thought communication could be improved at Cundari.

I hate it when I’m trying to log in somewhere and the keyboard covers the login button. So, I made sure to keep the login above the keyboard.

Jamieson Website

www.jamiesonvitamins.com

This is one the first big projects that I worked on, and it was a great learning experience for me. We took an outdated design and created a new e-commerce base while also improving aesthetics.

Because of brand colours we had to work within a strict limit. As well, in Canada, AODA is one of the guidelines that we really need to take it into consideration. We kept the site clean, but at the same time we managed to put a lot of information into the home page.

Our objective was to encourage people to make purchases, but at the same time we wanted to push them towards the blog, which is filled with plenty of relevant information.

Promescent

www.promescent.com

The Promescent website gave us a really good creative opportunity. The product was formulated by a urologist, and is used to help men last longer in bed.

So we made the home page really interactive and seamless. The scroll bottle on the left spins, so the product stays there throughout, and the content appears next to it with every scroll. The background has a really nice gradient effect and cool particle light effect.

The website is currently being populated with new copy.
